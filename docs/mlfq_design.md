We chose a three-level MLFQ design because testing showed a clear distinction between short, medium, and long-running processes without the added overhead of more queues. The time quanta were set to 10 for Q0 and 20 for Q1 to prioritize fast response for interactive tasks while still allowing longer jobs to make progress, with Q2 using FCFS to minimize context switching for CPU-bound processes. Allotment values of 50 and 160 were selected to give processes multiple opportunities to complete at higher priority levels before demotion, preventing premature classification as CPU-heavy. A boost period of 300 was introduced after observing starvation in long-running jobs, ensuring they periodically regain priority without significantly affecting responsiveness.
